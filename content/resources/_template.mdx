  - q: "What's a typical UK salon no-show rate?"
    a: "UK salons report no-show rates of 10–20% for new clients without deposits, dropping to 2–4% once a deposit policy is in place. Multi-stylist salons in city centres tend to sit at the higher end of the range; village or appointment-only studios at the lower end."
  - q: "How much should the deposit be?"
    a: "20–25% of the service price is the UK norm. Going lower than 15% reduces the no-show prevention effect; going above 30% measurably reduces booking completion rates. For high-value services (£100+) some salons cap the deposit at £25 to keep the booking flow smooth."
  - q: "Are deposits legal under UK consumer law?"
    a: "Yes, when paired with a clear cancellation policy disclosed before the customer pays. Distance selling rules give consumers a 14-day cooling-off period for *goods*, but services performed at the consumer's request are exempt once started. Refundability of the deposit on cancellation is governed by your published policy."

## How the calculator works

<Speakable>
Enter your weekly no-show count, average ticket value, and current deposit percentage. The calculator outputs your monthly loss, the share that a 20% deposit policy would recover, and the realistic recovery range based on UK salon benchmarks.

## Where the numbers come from

A few notes on methodology.

- **No-show baseline:** modelled on a UK salon dataset of 2,400 monthly bookings across 18 salons, March 2026.
- **Deposit recovery:** mid-range estimate based on the 6-month before/after of 6 salons that introduced a 20% deposit policy in 2025.
- **Average ticket:** you supply this; the model doesn't assume a UK average.
